SPH goes beyond education, focusing on academic excellence, faith development, and character growth. With a supportive community and passionate teachers, we create an environment where every child can thrive.

Michael Victor Sianipar
Class of 2007 | SPH Lippo Village
Vice Chairman of Partai Perindo | News Content Director iNews MNC | Chairman of Harian Federasi Futsal Indonesia | Chairman of Indonesian Youth Diplomacy
SPH Lippo Village was a challenging yet transformative experience.
Arriving in 2005 with limited English, I struggled academically, especially with History HL. However, supportive teachers helped me overcome difficulties, understand context without knowing every word, and convey messages with a limited vocabulary. Additionally, my daily bus commute to and from school exposed me to diverse social realities, sparking my social and life calling. A pivotal moment was a conversation with Mr. Brian Cox during a G12 retreat. His message, "Just Give Up," initially puzzled me, but he explained that SPH's education emphasizes relying on Christ's strength, not our own. As an SPH alumnus working in government and politics for 11 years, I reflect on the vision and mission instilled in me: to proclaim the pre-eminence of Christ and engage in the redemptive restoration of all things in Him through holistic education. This lesson is a cornerstone of my gratitude for SPH's impact on my life.

Nathaniel Gray Sutanto
Class of 2009 | SPH Lippo Village
Assistant Professor of Systematic Theology at Reformed Theological Seminary
A lot of schools are motivated by achieving high academic results. To them, numbers matter more than people. At SPH, people matter more than the numbers.
My time at SPH truly impacted me, and one of the highlights was the caring teachers and community. When I was at SPH, I was a delinquent student, to say the least. I never really listened to my teachers and often fell asleep in class. I now realize that even though I did not listen, a lot of teachers paid attention to me and cared for me. SPH is not a place that expels or rejects you. Instead, it is a community that pursues you because they truly care. They invite you back in to restore you. For example, Mr. Brian Cox, a former Head of School, was my ethics teacher. I once asked a rebellious question in his class. He didn’t shun me but instead invited me for coffee in his office to talk.

Gregory Kurnia
Class of 2016 | SPH Lippo Village
SPH has provided me with a comprehensive education that has been essential in shaping my life and career. Its rigorous academic program gave me a strong foundation of knowledge across various subjects, preparing me for real world situations.
I am particularly grateful for the depth and practical application of the curriculum, which equipped me with the skills necessary to succeed. Moreover, SPH’s emphasis on Christian values provided a solid moral compass that has guided me throughout my life. Beyond academics, SPH has developed a holistic approach to education, focusing on character development, work ethic, and overall well-being. This comprehensive education proved invaluable as I progressed through university, earned a master’s degree, and ultimately ventured into entrepreneurship. SPH’s influence is evident in every aspect of my life, and I am truly thankful that SPH has played a huge role in shaping me into who I am today.

Currently, I lead DEUS Human Capital Services, a startup dedicated to strategic HR management that serves diverse human resources needs across Indonesia. A highlight of my entrepreneurial journey is developing Indonesia's first locally based gamified HR assessments.

Mikhael Yosia
Class of 2009 | SPH Sentul City
Resident in the Otorhinolaryngology-Head and Neck Surgery Department at the Faculty of Medicine, Universitas Indonesia.
Attending SPH Sentul City and pursuing the International Baccalaureate (IB) program was a transformative experience that shaped both my personal and professional life. The rigorous and comprehensive IB curriculum challenged me to think critically and independently, fostering a deep-seated love for lifelong learning.
This foundation was instrumental in preparing me for the demanding academic environment at the Faculty of Medicine, Universitas Indonesia, where I pursued my medical degree and eventually became a General Practitioner. SPH not only provided a strong academic foundation but also nurtured my spiritual growth. The emphasis on faith at SPH helped me build resilience and maintain a positive outlook during challenging times. This aspect of my education has been invaluable, providing me with strength and guidance throughout my medical career. In addition to academic and spiritual development, SPH played a crucial role in honing my research skills and English proficiency. The ability to communicate effectively in English and conduct thorough research has been critical in my career. These skills have allowed me to engage with a global audience, including working with prestigious organizations such as Doctors Without Borders and Oxford University. My research work has been recognized internationally, with over 43 publications and more than 175 citations. Currently, I am a resident in the Otorhinolaryngology-Head and Neck Surgery Department at the Faculty of Medicine, Universitas Indonesia. The diverse experiences and solid foundation provided by SPH have been instrumental in my journey, enabling me to contribute to the global medical community.
